What's coming next...

Date Thu 01 November 2018 Tags Planning / Motivation

I'm going to start doing this regularly in 2019. I'm making this post now to give myself a bit of \(v_{0}\) going into the new year. A short list of what's coming:

  1. A while back I was messing with n-queens, and I found myself needing to test board permutations against the internal symmetries of chess. That lead me back to one of my favorite things to think about, generating permutations. This lead me to looking at Cayley graphs of \(D_4\), which lead me to bell ringing from the 17th century . I'll explain.

  2. Recently I found myself reading about belief propagation. This lead me to a 2017 paper from Chris Moore, talking about the hardness of certain graph problems in the setting of statistical physics and random graph theory. It lead me to thinking about why Facebook needs trolls. I'm sure this is well trodden ground, but I'll explain my thoughts.

  3. I want to write up how I've learned to think about weather, particularly how I think about the expected utility of purchasing a ski pass for Tahoe.

  4. I recently read on HackerNews the assertion that "Nakamoto Consensus" was a "CS breakthrough" in the Byzantine Generals Problem. That seems to me a bit strong, but I want to explore in 2018 how much of that statement is true. I'm also interested how many young engineers out there share this view.

  5. One of the things we think about (and obliquely work on), is the role of Machine Learning in entertainment and content creation. There's a narrative out there that Netflix told the creators of House of Cards to make a show with Kevin Spacey in it because they knew it would be a hit. That's not actually what happend. I'm interested in the history of this myth, vis-a-vis the rise of Big Data, and the AI, as SV hype tribes.

  6. Ultimately, I need to write about innovation. I've been running an innovation center for a $150B+ company for going on six years now. I have a lot of thoughts to share about innovation programs, innovation in general, and the future of R&D.
