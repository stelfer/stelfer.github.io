What I'm reading August 2016

Date Wed 03 August 2016 Tags Papers / Books

This is a list of things that held my attention long enough for me to honestly say I'm "reading" it.

Papers

Secure Multiparty Computation for Privacy-Preserving Data Mining
One of our interns this summer (Devangi) did her project on looking at differential privacy for machine learning and statistical models. I'm catching up on some of the things they learned about secure computation.
Secure Data Exchange: A Marketplace in the Cloud
Very interesting work from MSR. I'm not really in a position (yet?) to have an opinion about the protocols, but this is pretty exciting.
The Synthesis of Two Terminal Switching Circuits
With all of the reading on Yao Garbled circuits for SC, I somehow ended up with this gem from Shannon. One of the good things about teaching yourself theoretical computer science is that you get to discover things like this on your own!
