What I'm reading June 2016

Date Fri 03 June 2016 Tags Papers / Books

This is a list of things that held my attention long enough for me to honestly say I'm "reading" it.

Papers

The Evolution of the Markov Chain Monte Carlo Methods
My favorite introduction to Monte Carlo, and it gives a great overview from Metropolis-Hastings to Gibbs Sampling and MCMC
Learning to Communicate with Deep Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning
I'm very interested in building big multi-agent systems using distributed systems principles.
Analysis of Thompson Sampling for the multi-armed bandit problem
I've been playing with some ipnyb experiments of Thompson sampling vs UCB1
The Byzantine Generals Problem
I've been reading this in order to give PBFT another go.
The Bitcoin Backbone Protocol: Analysis and Applications
Slowly working my way into security proofs, but this looks way interesting
